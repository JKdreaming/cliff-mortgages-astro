Program Overview

DSCR Loans for Real Estate Investors

Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR) loans qualify investors based on a property’s rental income instead of personal tax returns. We move at an investor’s pace—running scenarios quickly, sourcing competitive DSCR programs for long- and short-term rentals, and mapping fees and cash flow so you can make smart, fast decisions.

Key Benefits

  • » Speed & Certainty: proactive timelines that keep your contract on track.
  • » Program Expertise: access to DSCR options tailored to your specific property strategy.
  • » Transparency for ROI: clear breakdowns of rates, terms, and fees so you can model cash-on-cash returns.
  • » Alternative qualification without W-2s, tax returns, or personal DTI requirements.
  • » Flexible structures for long-term rentals, short-term rentals, and portfolio growth.

Who is this for?

This loan may be a great fit if you meet the following general criteria:

  • » Investors purchasing or refinancing 1–4 unit income-producing properties.
  • » Borrowers building short-term or long-term rental portfolios.
  • » Clients with strong assets and credit who prefer alternative documentation.
  • » Entities and individuals seeking to close in the name of an LLC for liability management.

These are general guidelines. Contact us for a personalized assessment. Not a commitment to lend.

Why Investors Use DSCR Loans

DSCR loans qualify based on property income instead of personal tax returns. That means higher leverage, faster approvals, and flexibility for complex financial profiles.

  • Qualify using property cash flow, not personal DTI.
  • Close in personal name or LLC for liability management.
  • Great for expanding short-term rental portfolios.

Why Traditional Financing Can Fall Short

Conventional underwriting often penalizes investors for write-offs, multiple properties, or LLC ownership. DSCR programs bypass those limitations while keeping terms competitive.

  • Requires full tax returns and W-2/1099 documentation.
  • May cap property counts or require additional reserves.
  • Harder to close under an LLC structure.
