Program Overview

Colorado Home Equity Line of Credit (HELOC)

A Home Equity Line of Credit (HELOC) is a revolving line of credit—similar to a credit card—that lets you borrow as needed. It gives you flexibility to access money when you need it, making it a strong solution for upcoming projects, unexpected expenses, or consolidating higher-interest debt. With a HELOC, you only pay interest on the amount you draw, and once it’s repaid, the line becomes available to use again.

Key Benefits

  • » Borrow only what you need, when you need it, up to your approved credit limit.
  • » Pay interest only on the funds you draw, not the entire line.
  • » Often offers lower interest rates than personal loans or credit cards.
  • » Interest paid on a HELOC may be tax-deductible (consult a tax advisor).

Who is this for?

This loan may be a great fit if you meet the following general criteria:

  • » Colorado homeowners with sufficient equity in their property.
  • » Borrowers with stable income and good credit history.
  • » Individuals looking for flexible access to funds without a lump-sum loan.
  • » Homeowners planning improvements, debt consolidation, or managing larger expenses over time.

These are general guidelines. Contact us for a personalized assessment. Not a commitment to lend.

HELOC vs. Cash-Out vs. Personal Loan

Choose the right way to access your equity by comparing flexibility, payment structures, and costs.

HELOC

  • Best for: Short-term projects, flexible access.
  • Payment Type: Pay interest only on the amount you draw.
  • Key Feature: Revolving credit line for repeated use.

Cash-Out Refinance

  • Best for: Large, one-time projects or rate/term improvements.
  • Structure: Replaces your mortgage with a new fixed-rate loan.
  • Payment Type: Full principal and interest on entire new balance.

Personal Loan

  • Best for: Smaller loan amounts, no home equity needed.
  • Structure: Unsecured installment loan.
  • Payment Type: Typically higher, fixed monthly payments.
