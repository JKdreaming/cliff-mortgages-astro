Program Overview

Colorado FHA Home Loans

An FHA loan, insured by the Federal Housing Administration, is a cornerstone of accessible homeownership in America. It’s designed to help low-to-moderate-income borrowers who might not qualify for a conventional loan. With more lenient credit score and down payment requirements, FHA loans open the door for many first-time homebuyers and families to purchase a home. We are experienced in navigating FHA guidelines to help ensure a smooth and successful closing.

Key Benefits

  • » Low 3.5% down payment requirement makes homeownership more attainable.
  • » Flexible credit score guidelines are more forgiving than conventional loans.
  • » Down payment funds can come from gifts from family members.
  • » Sellers are permitted to contribute up to 6% of the purchase price toward closing costs.
  • » FHA loans are assumable, which can be an attractive feature for future buyers if rates rise.

Who is this for?

This loan may be a great fit if you meet the following general criteria:

  • » Borrowers with a credit score of 580 or higher (though some lenders go lower).
  • » Individuals who can make a down payment of at least 3.5%.
  • » Buyers purchasing a primary residence that meets FHA property standards.
  • » Borrowers with a steady employment history and verifiable income.

These are general guidelines. Contact us for a personalized assessment. Not a commitment to lend.

The FHA Advantage: Your Path to Homeownership

An FHA loan is designed to make buying a home accessible, especially if you have less-than-perfect credit or a smaller down payment.

FHA Loan

  • Low 3.5% Down Payment:
    Requirement makes homeownership more attainable.
  • Flexible Credit Scores:
    Guidelines are more forgiving than conventional loans.
  • Gift Funds Allowed:
    Your down payment may come entirely from a family gift.

Conventional Loan

  • Higher Down Payment Often Needed:
    3–5% down is possible, but often paired with higher credit score requirements.
  • Stricter Credit Requirements:
    Typically requires a credit score of 620 or higher.
  • Gift Fund Rules Can Be Stricter:
    Some programs require a portion of the buyer’s own funds.
